Why 2026

The macro setup has never been
more favorable for farmland.

01

The rate cycle is turning

As interest rates normalize, real assets benefit from falling discount rates. The same dynamic that drove real estate valuations in 2010–2020 is now setting up for agricultural land — with the added protection of food security as a structural floor.

02

Dollar dynamics amplify returns

Commodity prices — corn, soybeans, wheat — are priced globally in dollars. Shifts in dollar strength flow directly into farm income and land values. In the current macro environment, that creates a natural tailwind for U.S. agricultural assets.

03

Institutions are accelerating

Nuveen, TIAA, and Brookfield have been accumulating U.S. farmland for over a decade. As institutional demand compresses availability, individual accredited investors face a narrowing window to access this asset class at current valuations.

Why Farmland

Three reasons the world's smartest
capital keeps coming back to the soil.

01

Inelastic Demand

Global population grows. Arable land does not. The math has been favorable for 10,000 years and nothing in the current macro environment changes it.

02

Inflation Hedge

Farmland rents are tied to commodity prices and CPI. When inflation rises, so does the income the land generates. It is one of the few real assets that benefits from the environment that destroys others.

03

Institutional Access, Private Ticket
